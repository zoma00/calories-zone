Important current blocker
-------------------------
The bundle can be built, but Play Store upload is not complete until it is signed with your upload key.

Signing is configured to use these environment variables:
1. ZOMBA_UPLOAD_STORE_FILE
2. ZOMBA_UPLOAD_STORE_PASSWORD
3. ZOMBA_UPLOAD_KEY_ALIAS
4. ZOMBA_UPLOAD_KEY_PASSWORD

How to create the upload key
----------------------------
Run this locally:

keytool -genkeypair -v -storetype PKCS12 -keystore zomba-upload-key.jks -alias zomba-upload -keyalg RSA -keysize 2048 -validity 10000

Then export these variables before building release:

export ZOMBA_UPLOAD_STORE_FILE=/absolute/path/to/zomba-upload-key.jks
export ZOMBA_UPLOAD_STORE_PASSWORD=your-store-password
export ZOMBA_UPLOAD_KEY_ALIAS=zomba-upload
export ZOMBA_UPLOAD_KEY_PASSWORD=your-key-password

Then build the signed bundle:

./gradlew bundleRelease

Play Console checklist
----------------------
1. Create a new app in Google Play Console
2. Choose app name: Calories Zone
3. Set default language
4. Choose App or Game: App
5. Choose Free or Paid
6. Confirm declarations and create app

Store listing assets you need
-----------------------------
1. App name
2. Short description
3. Full description
4. App icon 512 x 512
5. Feature graphic 1024 x 500
6. At least 2 phone screenshots
7. Privacy policy URL

Recommended text direction for this app
---------------------------------------
Short description example:
Track calories, save your profile, and log meals with local offline tools.

Full description example:
Calories Zone helps you estimate calorie targets, save your profile on-device, and log meals locally. It is designed as a local-first nutrition tracker with simple calorie planning and meal logging.

Policy and content items to prepare
-----------------------------------
1. Privacy policy page
2. Data safety answers
3. Health disclaimer if you position it as guidance and not medical advice
4. Age rating questionnaire
5. Ads declaration if you add ads later
6. In-app purchases declaration if you add premium later

Testing before production
-------------------------
1. Upload the signed .aab to Internal testing first
2. Add tester emails
3. Install from Play testing link on a real Android phone
4. Check profile save, meal logging, and app launch after reinstall
5. Confirm no broken privacy-policy or store-listing links

Release flow
------------
1. Build signed bundle
2. Upload .aab to Internal testing
3. Fix any Play warnings
4. Complete store listing and policy forms
5. Roll out to Closed testing or Production

Payments for this app
---------------------
If you later sell digital features inside the app, such as premium meal analysis or premium AI coaching, you must use Google Play Billing.

Do not use a normal external payment gateway for digital in-app products on Google Play.

What you'll need to create a developer account for yourself
To create this account, you'll need:

An email address for your developer profile
This will be shown on Google Play as part of your public developer profile. You'll need to verify it by receiving a code. You can provide a different email address for app support queries, for each of your apps, once you've created your account. Learn more about developer verifications

A contact phone number and email address for Google
These will only be used by Google to contact you if we need to, and won't be shown on Google Play. You'll be asked to verify the email address during registration, and the phone number after you've created your account.

A form of payment
To pay the one-time USD 25 registration fee
