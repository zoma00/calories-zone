ZombaCal Health App Note

Important access note
The Health apps declaration form is usually not available until the Google Play developer account signup is completed.
That means you normally need to finish account registration and pay the 25 USD fee before Play Console opens the full App content section.

What this means
This is normal.
It does not mean the app is already rejected.
It only means Google does not open the declaration workflow before the developer account is activated.

Practical risk for this app
ZombaCal is a basic nutrition tracker, calorie calculator, and meal logger.
That is a much lower-risk category than a medical app.
The app should fit under Health and fitness with Nutrition and Weight Management.

Why similar calorie apps get approved
- They avoid medical claims
- They use a clear privacy policy
- They complete the Health apps declaration after signup
- They add a non-medical disclaimer
- They keep the first release simple

What to prepare before paying
1. Privacy policy
State that profile and meal data are stored locally on the device for the current version.

2. Safe app description
Use wording like calorie tracking, meal logging, nutrition goals, and weight management support.
Do not say diagnosis, treatment, cure, or prevention.

3. Non-medical disclaimer
Use this text:
"ZombaCal is not a medical device and does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any medical condition. Consult a qualified healthcare professional for medical advice."

4. Health declaration draft
Planned answer: Nutrition and Weight Management.
Do not select Medical Device unless the app later becomes a regulated medical product.

Lowest-risk launch plan
Release version 1 as a free app with:
- calorie calculation
- meal logging
- saved profile
- simple nutrition guidance

Delay these until later if possible:
- payment integration
- strong AI coaching claims
- medical or disease-related features

Bottom line
Yes, you will most likely need to complete signup and pay before Google opens the Health apps declaration form.
That is normal for Play Console.
For this app, the safer strategy is to prepare the required text first, then pay, then submit a simple first release.
