Calories Zone App Content Policy

Purpose
Calories Zone is a nutrition and wellness app for calorie calculation, meal logging, simple weight-management support, and general wellness guidance.

App position
- Health and fitness app
- Nutrition and Weight Management category
- Not a medical device
- Not a diagnostic, treatment, or disease-management tool

Allowed app content
- Calorie estimates
- Meal logging
- Nutrition goal tracking
- Weight-management support
- General wellness tips
- Generic educational content about food habits and daily tracking

Allowed store listing claims
- calorie calculator
- meal tracker
- nutrition tracking
- weight management support
- local profile and meal logging
- general wellness guidance

Prohibited claims
- diagnosing diseases or conditions
- treating, curing, or preventing medical conditions
- replacing a doctor, dietitian, or healthcare professional
- guaranteed weight-loss outcomes
- clinical-grade or doctor-grade advice
- disease-specific treatment advice

AI content rules
- AI output must be framed as general wellness guidance only.
- AI output must not be presented as medical, diagnostic, or professional nutrition advice.
- The first Play Store release should avoid strong marketing language such as AI doctor, AI dietitian, or expert medical coaching.

Required disclaimer
Use this text in the store listing and preferably inside the app:
"Calories Zone is not a medical device and does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any medical condition. Consult a qualified healthcare professional for medical advice."

Privacy and data position
- Current version stores profile and meal data locally on the device.
- The privacy policy must clearly explain local data storage.
- If cloud sync, analytics, or external AI services are added later, the privacy policy must be updated before release.

Permissions policy
- Request only permissions needed for core app functionality.
- Avoid unnecessary sensitive or health-related permissions.
- Remove unused permissions before submission.

Store listing policy
- Screenshots and description must match the actual app.
- Avoid medical wording, before-and-after health promises, or exaggerated outcomes.
- Keep the first release positioned as a simple nutrition tracker.

Health apps declaration guidance
- Planned declaration: Health and fitness -> Nutrition and Weight Management.
- Do not declare the app as a Medical Device unless it later becomes a regulated medical product.

Release blockers before paying or submitting
1. No privacy policy.
2. No non-medical disclaimer.
3. Store description contains medical or disease claims.
4. AI is marketed as expert or clinical advice.
5. Health apps declaration answers are inconsistent with the app.

Recommended launch path
Release version 1 as a free, local-only nutrition tracker.
Delay in-app billing, aggressive AI claims, and any medical-style features until after the first approval.

Submission decision
If the app keeps this scope, the Play review risk is manageable enough to prepare the documents first and then complete the developer signup.
