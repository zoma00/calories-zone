Calories Zone Rejection Risk Checks

Current risk level
Low to moderate, if the first Play Store release stays simple and avoids medical claims.

Why calorie apps still get approved
- They present themselves as wellness or nutrition tools, not medical products.
- They use safe store text.
- They include a privacy policy.
- They complete the Health apps declaration correctly.
- They do not request unnecessary sensitive permissions.

Main rejection risks for this app
1. Medical claims
High risk if the app says it diagnoses, treats, cures, prevents, or manages diseases.

2. Strong AI health claims
High risk if the AI is marketed as expert nutrition advice, diagnosis, or a replacement for a doctor or dietitian.

3. Missing or weak privacy policy
High risk if Play Console asks how user data is handled and the policy is missing or unclear.

4. Wrong Health apps declaration
Medium risk if the app is submitted as non-health when it clearly logs calories, meals, weight, or nutrition goals.

5. Risky store listing text
Medium risk if screenshots or descriptions promise health outcomes or clinical results.

6. Unnecessary permissions
Medium risk if the app asks for permissions that are not needed for local meal logging and profile storage.

Low-risk setup for version 1
- Calorie calculation
- Meal logging
- Saved local profile
- Simple nutrition goals
- Generic wellness tips

Avoid in version 1
- Disease-specific advice
- Medical claims
- Supplement or prescription content
- Payment integration for app features unless it is needed immediately
- Aggressive AI marketing claims

Safe wording to use
- calorie calculator
- meal tracker
- nutrition tracking
- weight management support
- general wellness tips

Wording to avoid
- treats obesity
- fixes metabolism
- diagnoses conditions
- doctor-level advice
- personalized medical treatment

What to prepare before submission
1. Privacy policy that says profile and meal data are stored locally on device.
2. Non-medical disclaimer for the store listing and optionally in the app.
3. Health apps declaration draft with Nutrition and Weight Management.
4. Final review of screenshots, description, and AI wording.

Payment integration note
This means payment integration for app features, not for developer console account creation.
The 25 USD fee is only the one-time Google Play developer signup fee.
It is safer to avoid in-app payments, subscriptions, or Google Play Billing in version 1 unless you really need them.

Recommended first release strategy
Submit the app first as a free, local-only nutrition tracker.
After the app is approved, add more features carefully.

Bottom line
The biggest risk is not the app category itself.
The biggest risk is how the app is described and whether it looks like a medical or regulated product.
